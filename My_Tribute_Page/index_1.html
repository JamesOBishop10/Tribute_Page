James O'Neil Bishop II

Naval veteran, Class A operator, Carolinas Handyman Solutions founder

Early Life and Career

James O'Neil Bishop II was born in Ann Arbor, Michigan, and spent his childhood between the shores of Michigan and the sunlit streets of Florida.
Before turning thirteen in 1984, he was already working construction sites, mastering the sheetrock trade with a craftsman's eye.
As he grew older, he joined a grocery chain, following a path familiar to many of his peers.

Active in both school and neighborhood sports, he bore the marks of youthful grit—scraped knees and torn palms from skateboarding, bruises from the track and wrestling mats.
He competed in mile relays, the 440-yard dash, high and long jumps, and ran three-mile races with relentless drive.
On the wrestling team, he grappled in the 145-pound division, and on the football field, he played with heart.
His passion for boxing and martial arts pushed him to accept the challenge of anyone willing to touch gloves.

Military Service

In 1988, at the age of 17, James enlisted in the United States Navy under the Delayed Entry Program - leaving for bootcamp in early April 1989, following in the footsteps of his father, who had served in the Navy during the Vietnam War.

U.S.S. King DDG-41

Drawn to the sea like his father—who served aboard the U.S.S. Kitty Hawk in the Pacific fleet—James enlisted in the Navy as a Machinist Mate on the U.S.S. King DDG-41, a guided missile destroyer.
There, he took charge of main communications during drills and emergencies, relaying vital information directly to the bridge.
As a member of “A-Gang,” he worked in the engine rooms, boiler rooms, emergency diesel generator spaces, and other critical engineering zones.
He stood watches, performed preventive maintenance, and cleaned tirelessly—stripping and waxing enough tile to cover all of Washington, D.C.
His enlistment ended with an honorable discharge in 1991, as the U.S.S. King DDG-41 was being readied to be decommissioned.

His naval service, combined with later roles as a Class A and heavy equipment operator, laid the groundwork for his journey into entrepreneurship—a path built on discipline, resilience, and the enduring will to forge his own legacy.
